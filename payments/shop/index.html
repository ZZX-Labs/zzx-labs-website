Shop Payment Policy

This page defines the payment-side policy for ZZX-Labs Shop purchases. It mirrors the shop payment page, but treats shop checkout as part of the broader Bitcoin payment system: invoices, receipts, confirmations, refunds, returns, fulfillment, delivery, and security verification.

Shop purchases are intended to be Bitcoin-only, with on-chain Bitcoin for larger settlement and Lightning Network payments for small, fast, recurring, subscription, API, or low-value purchases once fully deployed.

Scope

Applies to shop purchases, downloads, physical products, licenses, memberships, subscriptions, and custom shop orders.

Bitcoin Only

Shop payment rails are intended to use Bitcoin on-chain and Lightning Network settlement only.

Order-Specific Invoices

Final checkout should use unique order invoices, addresses, labels, or Lightning invoices.

Manual Review

Large, custom, restricted, high-value, or business purchases should be reviewed before payment.

Checkout Flow

Shop Checkout Payment Lifecycle

  1. 01 Cart Created:
    User selects products, licenses, downloads, physical goods, or service bundles.
  2. 02 Order Generated:
    The shop creates an order ID, item list, BTC price, fulfillment type, and invoice status.
  3. 03 Invoice Issued:
    The payment system generates an on-chain address, Lightning invoice, or manual invoice record.
  4. 04 User Verifies:
    User verifies address, QR code, invoice amount, official URL, expiration, and payment purpose.
  5. 05 Payment Detected:
    Payment monitoring detects the transaction or Lightning settlement.
  6. 06 Confirmation Policy Applied:
    Product-specific confirmation rules determine when fulfillment may begin.
  7. 07 Fulfillment Released:
    Digital items unlock, physical goods enter packing, or custom orders move into production.
  8. 08 Receipt Generated:
    Receipt, transaction ID, order reference, and delivery status are recorded.

Confirmations

Shop Confirmation Policy

Confirmation rules should not be identical for every shop product. The correct threshold depends on value, product type, reversibility, fraud risk, delivery method, and customer history.

Lightning Orders

Usually settled instantly once invoice payment succeeds, subject to invoice validity and system status.

Small Digital Orders

May use lower confirmation thresholds where delivery risk is acceptable.

Physical Orders

Should use stronger confirmation thresholds before packing and shipping.

High-Value Orders

May require manual review, additional confirmations, signed instructions, or escrow workflows.

Receipts

Receipts and Proof of Payment

Shop receipts should eventually contain order ID, invoice ID, item list, BTC amount, payment rail, payment address or invoice hash, transaction ID where applicable, confirmation state, delivery status, and timestamp.

Receipts should not expose unnecessary private information, wallet metadata, private notes, internal labels, or sensitive fulfillment details.

Security

Shop Payment Security

Shop payments are a high-risk target for phishing, fake invoices, copied QR codes, address substitution, malicious mirrors, fake support messages, and fraudulent order pages.

Never Pay From A Screenshot

Use official pages or signed instructions. Screenshots can be stale, edited, spoofed, or copied from phishing pages.

Compare QR and Text

Always confirm that the QR code resolves to the same address or Lightning invoice displayed in text.

Check Active Alerts

Review security alerts before large payments or suspicious checkout events.

Request Signed Instructions

For high-value shop or licensing payments, request GPG-signed payment instructions.
