Partnership Philosophy

Aligned Work, Not Empty Branding

ZZX-Labs is interested in partnerships that produce real work: tools, research, public infrastructure, documentation, code, datasets, prototypes, publications, security improvements, educational systems, and credible technical capacity.

We do not treat partnership as a logo exchange. A serious partnership should define what will be built, researched, published, funded, tested, deployed, secured, or maintained.

Research

Joint papers, reports, technical reviews, datasets, analysis, public-interest studies, and publication workflows.

Development

Software, APIs, dashboards, firmware, hardware, embedded systems, data tools, and open-source releases.

Infrastructure

Bitcoin nodes, Lightning concepts, server mirrors, analytics systems, mempool tools, node intelligence, and public data services.

Education

Technical training, public awareness, documentation, curriculum, workshops, guides, and knowledge-transfer systems.

Collaboration Models

How Partnerships Can Work

Joint Research

Shared papers, analysis, datasets, reports, reviews, experiments, disclosures, and technical documentation.

Joint Build

Co-developed tools, APIs, software, firmware, hardware prototypes, dashboards, widgets, and infrastructure services.

Incubation

Startup concepts, project commercialization, venture profiles, service formation, and partner-backed development tracks.

Sponsorship

Funding for specific papers, tools, datasets, public infrastructure, documentation, events, or development milestones.

Advisory

Technical review, security critique, legal/policy coordination, operational review, and strategy support.

Deployment

Infrastructure hosting, mirrors, nodes, testbeds, field trials, pilot systems, and public service deployment.

Boundaries

Legal, Ethical, and Security Review

Partnership work touching cybersecurity, intelligence, defense-adjacent systems, dual-use hardware, sensitive data, infrastructure access, public datasets, or restricted jurisdictions requires careful review before any implementation or disclosure.

ZZX-Labs reserves the right to decline partnerships that conflict with legal requirements, operational security, research integrity, ethical boundaries, privacy commitments, or the broader mission.

Proposal Format

What To Include

A strong partnership proposal should be specific. Include your organization or identity, technical domain, proposed collaboration, expected output, resources available, timeline, public/private posture, funding model, legal constraints, security needs, and preferred communication method.
