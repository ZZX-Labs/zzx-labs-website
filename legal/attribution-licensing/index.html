Attribution & Licensing

This page documents the attribution and licensing principles used by ZZX-Labs R&D for open-source software, third-party dependencies, fonts, images, icons, datasets, APIs, research sources, public references, documentation, and redistributable materials.

ZZX-Labs R&D intends to respect upstream licenses, preserve author credit, maintain clear reuse terms, and publish original open-source work with explicit licensing wherever possible.

1

General Attribution Principle

ZZX-Labs R&D intends to credit upstream creators, maintainers, projects, researchers, artists, data providers, documentation authors, and organizations whose work materially supports ZZX-Labs publications, tools, software, websites, datasets, and research.

Attribution may appear in repository license files, README files, documentation, source headers, credits pages, inline comments, footnotes, paper references, metadata files, or dedicated attribution records.

2

Open-Source Software

Open-source components used by ZZX-Labs R&D retain their original licenses. ZZX-Labs does not claim ownership over third-party open-source software merely because it is used, integrated, referenced, mirrored, wrapped, documented, patched, or redistributed.

Where ZZX-Labs releases original software, the preferred default license is the MIT License unless a project requires a different license because of upstream dependencies, legal review, partner agreements, or project purpose.

3

Repository-Level Licensing

Each public repository should include clear licensing information. Where applicable, repositories should contain:

  • LICENSE
  • README.md
  • NOTICE or ATTRIBUTION
  • THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES
  • SPDX license identifiers
  • Dependency manifests
  • Credits or acknowledgements

4

Third-Party Dependencies

Software dependencies, frameworks, libraries, packages, fonts, icons, datasets, models, APIs, and media assets may be governed by their own terms. Users redistributing ZZX-Labs projects are responsible for respecting those upstream licenses.

ZZX-Labs should not remove upstream copyright notices, license notices, attribution requirements, or required disclaimers from third-party materials.

5

Fonts, Icons, Logos, and Visual Assets

Fonts, icons, logos, images, diagrams, design files, and visual assets may have separate rights and licenses from source code. Some assets may be original ZZX-Labs work; others may be third-party assets, public-domain material, open-license material, commissioned work, or fair-use references.

Users should not assume that software licensing automatically grants permission to reuse all branding, logos, artwork, or visual identity materials.

6

Research Citations

Research pages, papers, reports, dossiers, analysis, commentary, and educational materials should cite sources where appropriate. Citations may include papers, books, datasets, public records, repositories, documentation, interviews, standards, advisories, news reports, archives, or official publications.

Citations should preserve context and should not imply endorsement by the cited author, project, or organization unless explicitly stated.

7

Datasets and APIs

Datasets and APIs may have access restrictions, attribution requirements, rate limits, redistribution limits, privacy restrictions, or licensing terms. ZZX-Labs R&D should document source, license, access date, and transformation status where feasible.

Derived datasets should distinguish between original source data, processed data, generated data, aggregated data, inferred data, and ZZX-Labs-created annotations.

8

Bitcoin and Blockchain Data

Bitcoin blockchain data, mempool data, node data, mining statistics, exchange data, and market data may be public, third-party sourced, locally mirrored, API-derived, or generated through ZZX-Labs analysis.

Market data, exchange data, and API-derived data may be subject to third-party terms. Users should verify licensing and redistribution permissions before republishing derived datasets commercially.

9

Documentation and Written Materials

Documentation, manuals, tutorials, papers, reports, guides, and educational materials may be licensed separately from software. If no explicit license is provided, users should not assume unrestricted reuse.

Future ZZX-Labs documents should clearly state whether they are open, restricted, draft, internal, copyrighted, public-domain, Creative Commons, MIT-licensed, or otherwise governed.

10

Trademark and Brand Use

ZZX-Labs names, logos, branding, marks, project names, visual identity, and trade dress may not be reused in a way that implies endorsement, sponsorship, partnership, official release status, or affiliation without written permission.

References to ZZX-Labs for criticism, commentary, citation, compatibility, security research, or factual identification should avoid confusion or misrepresentation.

11

Contribution Licensing

Contributors should only submit work they have the right to contribute. Contributions may be accepted under the license of the relevant repository or project unless a separate contributor agreement, assignment, or licensing rule applies.

Contributors should not submit third-party code, media, datasets, documentation, secrets, credentials, proprietary material, or restricted information unless properly authorized and licensed.

12

Fair Use and Commentary

Some materials may be referenced, quoted, excerpted, screenshotted, or discussed for commentary, critique, education, research, interoperability, archival, security, or documentation purposes. Those uses are covered separately by the Creative Fair Use page.

Attribution Record Format

Future attribution records may be stored as structured data. 

{
    "name": "Example Project",
    "type": "software-library",
    "url": "https://example.org/",
    "license": "MIT",
    "copyright": "Copyright holder",
    "used_in": [
        "example-tool"
    ],
    "attribution_required": true,
    "notes": ""
}

14

Corrections and Takedown Requests

If a rights holder believes that attribution is missing, incomplete, inaccurate, or that material has been used improperly, they may contact ZZX-Labs through the Contact page.

Valid requests should include the affected URL, rights holder identity, work description, evidence of ownership or authority, requested correction, and preferred contact method.
