Your Data

This page explains what rights users may have regarding information submitted to, collected by, generated through, or associated with ZZX-Labs R&D systems.

We believe individuals should understand what information exists, how it is used, and what options may be available regarding its correction, export, restriction, or deletion.

1

Right to Know

Users may request information regarding what personal data ZZX-Labs R&D may possess concerning them, subject to identity verification, security requirements, operational limitations, legal obligations, and technical feasibility.

2

Right to Access

Where technically feasible and legally permissible, users may request access to information associated with their interactions with ZZX-Labs systems.

  • Contact submissions
  • Application submissions
  • Volunteer requests
  • Partnership requests
  • Donation communications
  • Startup incubation submissions
  • Bug reports
  • Security disclosures

3

Right to Correction

Users may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information maintained by ZZX-Labs R&D.

Requests may require verification before changes are made.

4

Right to Deletion

Users may request deletion of certain personal information where legally permitted and operationally feasible.

Some information may be exempt from deletion because of:

  • Legal obligations
  • Security investigations
  • Fraud prevention
  • Operational requirements
  • Historical archives
  • Research preservation
  • Public records

5

Right to Export

Users may request a copy of information they submitted where technically feasible.

Export formats may include:

  • TXT
  • CSV
  • JSON
  • PDF
  • ZIP Archives

6

Public Information

Information intentionally published publicly by a user may remain publicly accessible even if a deletion request is later submitted.

Examples include:

  • Research publications
  • Public repositories
  • Blog comments
  • Forum posts
  • Open-source contributions
  • Public acknowledgements

7

Security Data Exceptions

Certain security-related information may not be disclosed, modified, exported, or deleted where doing so would compromise investigations, infrastructure security, abuse prevention, or legal obligations.

8

Bitcoin Information

Bitcoin addresses, transaction identifiers, blockchain records, and public ledger data generally cannot be modified or removed by ZZX-Labs because such information may exist permanently on public blockchain networks.

9

Identity Verification

Before fulfilling certain requests, ZZX-Labs may require reasonable verification to confirm the identity and authority of the requesting party.

10

Future Account Systems

Future account platforms, research portals, startup incubation systems, consulting portals, secure communications systems, and APIs may introduce additional user controls and privacy tools.

11

Policy Updates

This document may evolve as services evolve. Future revisions will be versioned and archived through the Legal Hub.
