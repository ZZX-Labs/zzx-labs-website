Data Policy

This Data Policy defines how ZZX-Labs R&D collects, creates, classifies, stores, processes, secures, publishes, archives, transfers, and destroys information.

This policy applies to public websites, research repositories, software releases, documentation portals, APIs, startup incubation systems, communications platforms, payment systems, analytics infrastructure, and future services operated by ZZX-Labs R&D.

Data Classification

ZZX-Labs classifies information into the following categories:

  • Public Data
  • Published Research Data
  • Open Source Project Data
  • Operational Data
  • Administrative Data
  • Financial Data
  • Security Data
  • Analytics Data
  • Archived Data
  • Confidential Data
  • Restricted Data
  • Partner Data
  • Incubation Program Data
  • Future Customer Data

2

Data Sources

Data may originate from users, contributors, researchers, volunteers, donors, consultants, partners, applicants, public blockchains, public datasets, APIs, automated systems, operational infrastructure, and internally generated research.

3

Public Data

Information intentionally published through websites, Git repositories, software releases, research papers, technical documentation, public APIs, RSS feeds, blog posts, and downloads is considered Public Data.

Public Data may remain archived indefinitely for historical, research, educational, and transparency purposes.

4

Security Data

Security logs, abuse reports, attack records, threat intelligence, bug bounty submissions, disclosure reports, and infrastructure monitoring data may be collected and retained to protect ZZX-Labs infrastructure and users.

5

Research Data

Research materials may include papers, datasets, notes, diagrams, software, models, experiments, benchmarks, and related technical artifacts.

Research data may be retained indefinitely for reproducibility, historical preservation, and scientific recordkeeping.

6

Data Storage

Data may be stored on self-hosted infrastructure, local servers, encrypted backups, cloud providers, Git repositories, archival media, and future distributed storage systems.

7

Retention and Archival

Different categories of data may have different retention schedules depending upon legal, operational, security, research, historical, and business requirements.

Certain information may be archived permanently.

8

Data Destruction

Where deletion is appropriate, data may be removed, anonymized, pseudonymized, encrypted, archived, or destroyed according to operational requirements and applicable law.

9

Future Services

Future account systems, private communications, secure storage services, startup incubation tools, consulting portals, payment systems, APIs, and collaboration platforms may introduce additional data categories and controls.

10

Policy Updates

This Data Policy may evolve as ZZX-Labs expands. Future revisions will be versioned, archived, and linked through the Legal Hub.

Questions

Data Policy Questions

Questions regarding data collection, storage, retention, classification, publication, archival, or destruction may be directed through the Contact page.
