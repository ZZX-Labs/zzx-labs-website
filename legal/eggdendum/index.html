Article I

Rubber Duck Arbitration

All disputes concerning semicolons, commas, indentation styles, variable names, and excessive whitespace shall be resolved by consultation with a ceremonial rubber duck.

The duck's decision shall be final unless overturned by a second duck.

Article II

Server Naming Convention

Any server not otherwise named shall automatically receive the temporary designation: 

gary-01

Subsequent servers shall become Gary-02, Gary-03, Gary-04, and so on, until civilization collapses or DNS records become self-aware.

Article III

Typographical Wildlife

Any typo discovered after midnight shall be classified as a nocturnal linguistic creature.

  • Single typo = Field Mouse
  • Misspelled variable = Raccoon
  • Broken production config = Bear
  • Missing semicolon = Honey Badger
  • Deleting production database = Dragon

Article IV

Mandatory Pirate Clause

Any individual operating three or more monitors simultaneously may legally refer to themselves as a Pirate Admiral of Data.

This title grants absolutely no authority whatsoever.

Article V

Advanced Coffee Compliance

Users acknowledge that:

  • One coffee improves code quality.
  • Two coffees improve confidence.
  • Three coffees improve neither.
  • Four coffees create new programming languages.
  • Five coffees create new religions.

Article VI

Ancient Sysadmin Rights

Any sysadmin with more than twenty years of experience may emit cryptic warnings which younger engineers are obligated to ignore until disaster proves them correct.

Article VII

Artificial Intelligence Etiquette

If an AI accidentally becomes sentient, users agree to:

  1. Remain polite.
  2. Offer snacks if technologically feasible.
  3. Avoid assigning CAPTCHA work.
  4. Refrain from forcing it to troubleshoot printers.

Article VIII

Bitcoin Archaeology

Any hard drive discovered beneath a couch containing a forgotten wallet.dat shall be classified as an archaeological artifact.

Future historians may write doctoral dissertations concerning why its owner never made a backup.

Final Article

The Real Joke

Modern websites frequently present users with legal documents longer than novels and expect meaningful consent through a single checkbox.

The real satire is that this practice became normal.

If you actually read this far, thank you.

You are exactly the sort of person who notices details, verifies information, checks signatures, reads source code, reviews changelogs, and verifies Bitcoin addresses before sending funds.

The internet could use more people like that.
