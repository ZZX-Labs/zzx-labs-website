Creative Fair Use

This page explains the general basis on which ZZX-Labs R&D may reference, quote, excerpt, cite, screenshot, transform, analyze, discuss, critique, or archive portions of third-party works for research, commentary, education, scholarship, interoperability, security review, public-interest analysis, and documentation.

This page is not legal advice. Fair use is fact-specific, jurisdiction-dependent, and should be reviewed carefully when uncertainty exists.

1

Purpose

ZZX-Labs R&D may discuss third-party materials in order to explain technology, document behavior, compare systems, critique claims, preserve context, conduct research, teach concepts, analyze security, review interoperability, or comment on public-interest issues.

Such uses may involve limited excerpts, screenshots, citations, thumbnails, quotations, diagrams, metadata, public records, public statements, public documentation, public repository references, or other contextual materials.

2

Transformative Use

ZZX-Labs R&D aims for third-party references to be transformative where possible. A transformative use adds new meaning, explanation, criticism, research value, educational context, technical analysis, interoperability insight, or public-interest commentary rather than merely republishing the original work.

3

Commentary and Critique

Materials may be referenced for criticism, commentary, comparative analysis, editorial discussion, technical explanation, security review, policy analysis, cultural discussion, or public-interest reporting.

Where feasible, ZZX-Labs should preserve source context and avoid misleading edits or quotations.

4

Education, Scholarship, and Research

Excerpts, citations, screenshots, and references may be used to support education, scholarship, research, documentation, reproducibility, technical explanation, historical recordkeeping, or scientific discussion.

Such uses should be limited to what is reasonably necessary for the educational or research purpose.

5

Interoperability and Technical Documentation

ZZX-Labs may reference third-party interfaces, APIs, file formats, protocols, documentation, schemas, screenshots, command outputs, behavior descriptions, or compatibility notes for interoperability and technical documentation.

Interoperability references should avoid unnecessary copying and should credit sources where appropriate.

6

Security Research

Security research may require limited discussion of third-party systems, vulnerabilities, behaviors, logs, screenshots, advisories, malware indicators, public artifacts, or technical documentation.

ZZX-Labs should avoid publishing exploit-enabling detail where it would create unnecessary risk before responsible disclosure or remediation.

7

Archival and Historical Context

Materials may be referenced to preserve historical context, document public claims, compare versions, analyze changes over time, or support public-interest recordkeeping.

Archival references should respect applicable rights and avoid substituting for the original market where reasonable alternatives exist.

8

Attribution

ZZX-Labs should provide attribution where reasonable, including author, project name, source URL, license, publication date, archive date, or other relevant identifiers.

Attribution does not necessarily imply endorsement by the original author or rights holder.

9

Limits

ZZX-Labs should avoid excessive copying, unnecessary republication, misleading excerpts, commercial substitution, removal of attribution, publication of private material, or misuse of third-party works beyond what is reasonably connected to research, commentary, critique, education, scholarship, interoperability, or public-interest analysis.

10

Rights Holder Requests

Rights holders may contact ZZX-Labs if they believe material has been used improperly, lacks attribution, requires correction, exceeds fair use, violates license terms, or should be removed.

Requests should include the affected URL, work description, rights holder identity, authority to act, claimed issue, and requested remedy.
