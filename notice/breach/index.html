Disclosure Policy

When A Breach Is Published

A breach disclosure should only occur after sufficient investigation confirms unauthorized access, compromise, disclosure, destruction, alteration, theft, corruption, or exposure of protected systems, services, infrastructure, or private data.

The goal is accurate disclosure rather than speculative disclosure.

Incident Categories

Disclosure Types

Infrastructure

Unauthorized access to servers, services, networking equipment, storage systems, cloud systems, or operational infrastructure.

Credentials

Exposure or compromise of passwords, tokens, API keys, SSH keys, or authentication mechanisms.

Signing Keys

Exposure, theft, loss of control, compromise, or suspected compromise of release-signing keys.

Data

Unauthorized disclosure, modification, corruption, or destruction of protected data.
