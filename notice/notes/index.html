Laboratory Notes

Notes serve as a public laboratory notebook for ideas, observations, planning discussions, architectural concepts, design considerations, research fragments, implementation thoughts, and future opportunities.

Not every note becomes a project. Not every project becomes a product. Not every product becomes a company. This page exists to preserve ideas before they are forgotten.

Purpose

Why Notes Exist

During research and development it is common to encounter ideas that are valuable but not yet actionable.

Rather than burying those ideas in private files, they can be preserved here for future review, expansion, implementation, publication, or retirement.

Architecture Notes

Site Architecture Observations

  1. NOTE Projects should become the source of truth.
    Most content can be derived from project records.
  2. NOTE Generated content scales better.
    Manually maintaining duplicate content creates drift.
  3. NOTE Documentation should be first-class.
    Documentation often outlives software releases.
  4. NOTE Static-first architecture remains desirable.
    Simplicity improves reliability and longevity.

Design Notes

User Experience Observations

  • Users should always know where they are.
  • Navigation should remain predictable.
  • Pages should be readable without JavaScript.
  • Typography should prioritize clarity.
  • Cards should summarize before linking deeper.
  • Important actions should remain visible.
  • Consistency reduces confusion.
  • Content should age gracefully.
