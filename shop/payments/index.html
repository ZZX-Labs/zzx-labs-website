Shop Payments

This page explains how Bitcoin payments are intended to work inside the ZZX-Labs Shop for digital downloads, software, hardware kits, books, publications, datasets, apparel, stickers, custom work, licenses, memberships, subscriptions, and future product releases.

The shop is Bitcoin-only by design. On-chain Bitcoin is intended for larger settlement and Lightning Network payments are intended for smaller, faster, recurring, subscription, and API-style payments once fully deployed.

Order Flow

How Shop Payment Should Work

  1. 01 Select Item:
    User selects a shop item such as software, hardware, publication, dataset, license, apparel, or custom work.
  2. 02 Create Order:
    Checkout creates an order record with item list, BTC price, order ID, payment rail, expiration, and delivery type.
  3. 03 Generate Invoice:
    A unique on-chain address or Lightning invoice is generated for that order.
  4. 04 Verify Payment Details:
    User verifies address, QR code, invoice amount, expiry, official URL, and payment purpose before sending Bitcoin.
  5. 05 Confirm Payment:
    Payment is detected and reaches the required confirmation threshold for that product class.
  6. 06 Fulfill Order:
    Digital items unlock downloads. Physical items enter packing and shipping. Custom work moves into the work queue.
  7. 07 Issue Receipt:
    A receipt or transaction record is generated for the user and for accounting records.

Fulfillment

Digital and Physical Delivery

Digital Downloads

Software, PDFs, reports, datasets, media, templates, and digital assets should unlock after payment confirmation according to product-specific rules.

Software Releases

Software downloads should include checksums and, where appropriate, detached signatures or release verification instructions.

Physical Goods

Hardware kits, apparel, prints, stickers, accessories, and collectibles should move to packing after required confirmation thresholds are met.

Custom Work

Custom work, licenses, consulting bundles, and private deliverables may require manual review, quoted invoices, milestones, or signed terms before payment.

Confirmations

Payment Confirmation Rules

Confirmation requirements may vary by item type, value, delivery risk, fraud risk, shipping status, service type, and payment rail. The old rule of waiting for a fixed high number of confirmations for every shop order is not ideal. The final system should use tiered confirmation rules.

Lightning

Generally instant once invoice settlement succeeds, subject to invoice validity and fraud checks.

Small Digital Orders

May require fewer confirmations depending on risk tolerance and delivery reversibility.

Physical Goods

Should wait for stronger confirmation before packing, shipping, or releasing expensive items.

High-Value Orders

May require more confirmations, manual review, signed instructions, or multisig/escrow workflows.

Final confirmation rules should be documented in Payment Policies and reflected on each invoice.

Security

Payment Security Rules

Verify The Address

Compare the complete address text before sending. Do not rely only on the first and last characters.

Verify The QR Code

Scan the QR code and confirm it decodes to the exact expected address or invoice.

Verify The Order

Confirm item list, amount, expiry, order ID, invoice ID, delivery method, and payment purpose.

Verify The Domain

Do not pay from screenshots, reposts, unknown mirrors, unofficial messages, or third-party claims.

Check Alerts

Review active alerts for phishing, fake shop pages, fraudulent addresses, or signing-key emergencies.

Ask Before Large Payments

Contact ZZX-Labs before large, custom, restricted, investment, grant, or high-value shop payments.

Infrastructure

Future Shop Payment Infrastructure

The final shop payment system should use Bitcoin-native infrastructure with minimal trust in intermediaries. The target design may include Bitcoin Core, LND, BTCPay Server, watch-only accounting, multisig treasury storage, signed invoices, cold storage, per-product labels, and per-order payment records.

BTCPay Server

Self-hosted merchant checkout for invoices, shop orders, receipts, and payment monitoring.

Bitcoin Core

Full-node verification for payment detection and Bitcoin network sovereignty.

LND

Lightning Network infrastructure for fast small-value checkout and subscriptions.

Watch-Only Accounting

Monitoring addresses without exposing private keys to web or shop infrastructure.

Signed Invoices

High-value payment instructions should support GPG signature verification.

Cold Storage

Larger balances should be moved away from hot wallets into secure treasury workflows.
