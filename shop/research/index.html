Methodology

Research Workflow

  1. 01Question:
    Define the research question, hypothesis, use case, risk, product, or technical problem.
  2. 02Scope:
    Set boundaries, deliverables, timeline, sources, data access, legal limits, and safety constraints.
  3. 03Collection:
    Gather public data, technical references, code, documentation, datasets, measurements, and observations.
  4. 04Analysis:
    Evaluate evidence, compare options, test assumptions, model systems, and identify constraints.
  5. 05Draft:
    Create the report, diagrams, tables, code notes, methodology, findings, and recommendations.
  6. 06Review:
    Check for accuracy, safety, citations, legal exposure, privacy, reproducibility, and clarity.
  7. 07Delivery:
    Provide the final paper, report, dataset, appendix, files, changelog, and licensing notes.

Integrity

Research Integrity and Limitations

Research deliverables should distinguish facts from assumptions, cite sources where available, identify uncertainty, avoid unsupported claims, and document limitations. Some research may become outdated as software, hardware, laws, markets, threats, and technical standards change.

Security, OSINT, legal, medical, adult-sector, financial, defense, and compliance-adjacent research may require additional review before publication or delivery.
