Gift Cards

The ZZX-Labs Gift Cards section is a Bitcoin-paid sourcing service for common, popular retail gift cards. The buyer pays ZZX-Labs in Bitcoin. ZZX-Labs purchases the requested gift card through lawful retail channels, retains the receipt record, applies a percentage premium, and delivers the card details or physical card according to the order terms.

Gift card availability, denominations, purchase limits, delivery method, verification requirements, premium rate, and eligibility may vary by retailer, jurisdiction, fraud-risk profile, and compliance review.

Service Model

Bitcoin-Paid Gift Card Sourcing

Customer Request

The buyer requests a common retail gift card, denomination, delivery type, and destination requirements.

Premium Quote

ZZX-Labs provides a BTC quote that includes card value, premium rate, BTC volatility buffer, fees, and handling.

Receipt-Based Purchase

Gift cards are purchased through lawful retail channels, with receipt records retained for order verification.

Delivery

Cards may be delivered digitally, physically, or through retailer-specific delivery methods where available.

Premiums

Premium Rate Structure

Each gift card order carries a percentage premium over face value. The premium compensates for sourcing, time, BTC-to-fiat conversion exposure, purchase friction, fraud screening, payment confirmation risk, delivery handling, and record keeping.

Standard Premium

Default percentage markup for ordinary, low-risk, common gift card requests.

Rush Premium

Higher premium for time-sensitive orders, same-day handling, or difficult sourcing.

High-Value Premium

Additional review and pricing for larger denominations or bulk orders.

Special-Order Premium

Custom premium for uncommon retailers, unusual delivery needs, or retailer-specific limitations.

Verification

Receipts, Records, and Compliance

Gift card sales are fraud-sensitive. ZZX-Labs may require verification, order review, payment confirmation, identity or contact review, region checks, retailer checks, fraud screening, or refusal of suspicious orders.

Receipt Record

Purchase receipts may be retained for verification, accounting, dispute review, and order history.

BTC Confirmation

Orders may not be purchased until Bitcoin payment has sufficient confirmation.

Fraud Review

Suspicious, high-risk, high-value, or unusual orders may be delayed, declined, or manually reviewed.

Legal Limits

Availability may depend on jurisdiction, retailer policy, gift card terms, payment rules, and compliance requirements.
