Workflow

Custom Work Process

  1. 01Request:
    Submit the project idea, goal, required deliverables, preferred timeline, budget range, and technical context.
  2. 02Scope:
    Define what will be built, what will not be built, assumptions, constraints, dependencies, and acceptance criteria.
  3. 03Review:
    Check legality, safety, feasibility, privacy, security, data handling, licensing, payment structure, and operational risk.
  4. 04Proposal:
    Prepare a written project plan, milestones, deliverables, estimated costs, payment terms, and communication workflow.
  5. 05Payment:
    Issue Bitcoin payment instructions, invoice, retainer, milestone payment, or other approved BTC-only structure.
  6. 06Build:
    Develop the software, hardware, firmware, data product, documentation, design asset, research output, or system.
  7. 07Review:
    Test, revise, document, verify, package, sign, hash, and prepare the deliverable for client review.
  8. 08Delivery:
    Deliver the final files, source code, documentation, license, invoice record, support notes, and next-step recommendations.

Requirements

What To Include In A Request

Project Goal

What needs to exist at the end of the project, and what problem it solves.

Deliverables

Files, code, apps, reports, datasets, designs, hardware, documentation, APIs, or support expected.

Technical Context

Existing repo, stack, platform, operating system, hardware, dependencies, data sources, and constraints.

Timeline

Desired deadline, priority, milestones, review cadence, urgency, and launch dependency.

Budget

Expected budget range, payment structure, deposit ability, retainer interest, and BTC payment preference.

Access

Whether private files, repos, servers, APIs, data, credentials, or test systems will be required.

License Needs

Personal, commercial, open-source, private, enterprise, redistribution, exclusive, or source-code rights.

Risk Notes

Privacy, security, legal, adult-sector, financial, hardware, firmware, safety, or compliance considerations.

Boundaries

Review, Safety, and Refusal Conditions

Custom work must be lawful, safe, technically feasible, and consistent with ZZX-Labs operating boundaries. Some requests may require legal review, security review, privacy review, compliance review, or written authorization before work can begin.

Lawful Use

Work must be intended for lawful projects, authorized systems, legitimate businesses, and permitted use cases.

Security Authorization

Security testing, OSINT, data work, and dual-use tooling may require proof of authorization and clear scope.

Privacy Protection

Projects must respect privacy, minimize sensitive data, protect credentials, and follow data-handling rules.

Hardware Safety

Hardware and firmware work may require extra review for power, RF, batteries, mechanical risk, and field use.

Jurisdiction Limits

Some regions, industries, exports, services, or clients may require approval or be declined.

No Unauthorized Harm

Requests involving unauthorized intrusion, theft, fraud, evasion, harassment, or abuse are not accepted.
