Research Archive

The ZZX-Labs Research Archive preserves older research materials, retired drafts, superseded papers, historical reports, previous dataset versions, release snapshots, deprecated technical notes, older disclosures, manifests, hashes, signatures, and long-term research records.

Archive records are not always current. Some may be superseded, corrected, deprecated, incomplete, or retained only for provenance, history, reproducibility, citation, and version-tracking.

Archive Classes

Archived Material Types

Superseded Papers

Older paper versions replaced by newer versions, corrected editions, or formal releases.

Historical Reports

Reports preserved for context, project history, older findings, and prior measurements.

Dataset Versions

Previous dataset snapshots, retired archives, older manifests, hash records, and data-release history.

Retired Drafts

Drafts no longer maintained but preserved to document research development and historical reasoning.

Older Disclosures

Closed advisories, remediation records, security notices, and disclosure timelines retained for reference.

Release Snapshots

Frozen copies of files, source bundles, site states, tool versions, documentation, and release artifacts.

Archive Status

Record Status Labels

Archived

The record is preserved but no longer treated as the current version.

Superseded

A newer version, paper, report, dataset, disclosure, or release has replaced this item.

Deprecated

The record should not be used for current decisions without checking newer material.

Historical

The item is retained for historical context, research continuity, or citation traceability.

Retracted

The item has been formally withdrawn, corrected, invalidated, or removed from current use.

Frozen

The item is intentionally preserved exactly as released, with no further edits planned.

Preservation

Archive Integrity

Canonical Record

Each archived item should point to a stable canonical URL or original release location where possible.

Hash Records

SHA256 hashes should be preserved for PDF files, archives, datasets, source bundles, and manifests.

Signatures

Important archive records may include signatures or signed manifests for stronger verification.

Replacement Links

Superseded records should link to the current version, correction, retraction, or replacement record.

Reason Archived

Records should explain whether they are outdated, replaced, corrected, frozen, retired, or kept for history.

Access State

Archive records may be public, private, member-only, client-only, restricted, or removed from public access.
