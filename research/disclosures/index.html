Disclosures

The ZZX-Labs Disclosures library is the research hub for responsible disclosures, vulnerability reports, public advisories, remediation records, technical notices, affected-system summaries, coordinated disclosure timelines, and security-related publication records.

This page is intended for lawful defensive security work. It should document issues clearly, avoid reckless publication, preserve technical accuracy, respect remediation timelines, and provide enough information for affected parties, researchers, users, and maintainers to understand the nature and status of a disclosure.

Disclosure Types

Disclosure Collections

Responsible Disclosures

Privately reported security findings coordinated with affected maintainers, vendors, clients, or project owners before public release.

Errata / Corrections

Corrections to prior advisories, updated impact statements, clarified scope, revised timelines, and amended findings.

Lifecycle

Disclosure Status Model

Received

A report has been received but not yet validated, triaged, reproduced, or assigned.

Triage

The issue is being reviewed for scope, evidence, severity, affected systems, and responsible handling.

Validated

The issue has been reproduced or otherwise confirmed with enough confidence for remediation planning.

Coordinating

ZZX-Labs is coordinating with affected parties, maintainers, clients, vendors, or internal project owners.

Mitigating

A workaround, configuration change, access restriction, patch, or operational mitigation is being applied.

Patched

A fix or mitigation has been released, deployed, or otherwise made available.

Published

A public advisory, disclosure note, or summary has been released with appropriate detail.

Closed

The issue is resolved, rejected, out of scope, superseded, or archived with final status notes.

Severity

Severity and Risk Labels

Disclosure records should include severity only when ZZX-Labs has enough evidence to assign it. Severity should not be exaggerated. Impact, exploitability, scope, exposure, affected users, available mitigations, and real-world risk should be considered together.

Informational

Useful security note, hardening suggestion, documentation issue, or low-risk observation.

Low

Limited impact, difficult exploitation, narrow scope, minor exposure, or easy mitigation.

Medium

Meaningful security impact with practical but constrained exploitation or limited affected scope.

High

Serious impact, realistic exploitation, meaningful exposure, important remediation need, or broad affected scope.

Critical

Severe impact, direct compromise risk, widespread exposure, destructive potential, or urgent remediation requirement.

Pending

Severity not assigned yet because evidence, impact, scope, or reproduction details are incomplete.

Safe Reporting

Submitting A Security Finding

Security findings should be reported privately. Include enough detail for validation without causing unnecessary harm. Do not access, modify, destroy, exfiltrate, publish, or misuse data. Do not test systems outside authorized scope. Do not perform denial-of-service testing. Do not exploit beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue safely.

Summary

Briefly describe the issue, affected system, expected impact, and why it matters.

Scope

State the affected URL, repository, software version, device, API, configuration, or environment.

Evidence

Provide safe screenshots, logs, proof notes, reproduction conditions, timestamps, and non-destructive validation steps.

Reproduction

Include minimal safe steps to reproduce. Avoid instructions that enable broad misuse if the issue is not yet fixed.

Impact

Explain what an attacker could plausibly do, what data or systems are affected, and what assumptions are involved.

Contact

Include a secure contact method, preferred attribution name, disclosure expectations, and coordination needs.

Advisory Metadata

Recommended Disclosure Record Fields

Identity

Advisory ID, title, slug, affected project, affected component, reporter, maintainer, and canonical URL.

Classification

Disclosure type, severity, status, access level, domain, tags, affected versions, and fixed versions.

Timeline

Reported date, acknowledged date, validated date, patched date, publication date, update dates, and closure date.

Technical Detail

Impact summary, root cause, affected systems, reproduction notes, evidence notes, and mitigation steps.

Remediation

Patch notes, configuration changes, workaround, upgrade path, fixed version, and verification status.

Verification

HTML advisory, PDF advisory, manifest, SHA256 hash, GPG signature, references, and release notes.

Disclosure Rules

Publication Discipline

Coordinate First

Attempt private coordination with affected parties before public disclosure whenever practical.

Minimize Harm

Avoid publishing unnecessary exploit detail, sensitive data, credentials, private information, or destructive steps.

Be Accurate

Do not overstate impact, invent affected scope, omit important limitations, or exaggerate severity.

Version Everything

Advisories should include dates, versions, update history, correction notes, and supersession records.

Protect Researchers

Credit researchers where appropriate, respect anonymity requests, and preserve safe reporting channels.

Preserve Records

Maintain hashes, manifests, advisories, timelines, and archive copies where reasonable.
