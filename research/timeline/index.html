Research Timeline

The Research Timeline provides chronological navigation across ZZX-Labs papers, reports, disclosures, analysis, datasets, projects, releases, revisions, milestones, archive events, and publication history.

Timeline records help readers understand when work began, when evidence was collected, when findings were published, when corrections were made, when disclosures were patched, and when releases were archived.

Timeline Events

Event Types

Research Started

Project, paper, report, dataset, analysis, or disclosure work begins.

Draft Created

A working draft, outline, prototype, dataset, note, or preliminary record is created.

Data Collected

Measurements, logs, samples, screenshots, sources, records, or datasets are collected.

Review Started

Internal review, technical review, security review, citation review, or editorial review begins.

Published

Paper, report, analysis, dataset, disclosure, project release, or tool is published.

Updated

Record is revised, corrected, expanded, amended, reissued, or replaced.

Patched / Remediated

Disclosure, vulnerability, issue, or security finding is fixed, mitigated, or closed.

Archived

Record is frozen, superseded, deprecated, retired, or preserved for historical reference.

Timeline Views

Ways To Browse The Timeline

By Date

Chronological list of all research events from newest to oldest or oldest to newest.

By Project

Timeline grouped by individual project, tool, dataset, report series, or disclosure series.

By Record Type

Separate timelines for papers, reports, disclosures, analysis, datasets, tools, and archive records.

By Author

Publication and contribution timeline for authors, contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and analysts.

By Tag

Timeline filtered by subject tag such as Bitcoin, OSINT, cybersecurity, AI, hardware, or policy.

By Status

Draft, review, published, updated, corrected, patched, deprecated, superseded, or archived records.

Timeline Metadata

Recommended Event Fields

Identity

Event ID, title, slug, event type, record type, related record, and canonical URL.

Date

Event date, date precision, timestamp, timezone, period start/end, and ordering key.

Context

Summary, reason for event, related project, related author, tags, and affected records.

Links

Related paper, report, analysis, disclosure, dataset, project, archive record, download, or source URL.

Verification

Manifest, hash, signature, release note, commit hash, citation, screenshot, or supporting evidence.

Visibility

Public, private, member-only, client-only, internal, restricted, or archived timeline entry.
