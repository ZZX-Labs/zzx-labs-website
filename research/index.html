Research Workflow

How Research Moves Through The Portal

Question

Research begins with a technical, strategic, scientific, security, policy, Bitcoin, OSINT, software, hardware, or data question.

Collection

Sources, measurements, logs, datasets, screenshots, references, code, experiments, and field notes are gathered.

Analysis

Collected material is interpreted to identify patterns, risks, opportunities, trends, failures, relationships, and implications.

Report

Findings are written into structured reports, technical reviews, briefs, market summaries, or research deliverables.

Paper

Formal concepts, systems, proposals, and methodologies are developed into white papers or technical papers.

Disclosure

Security findings are handled through responsible disclosure, remediation, advisory publication, and timeline preservation.

Dataset

Supporting data is packaged with metadata, licenses, manifests, hashes, signatures, and reproducibility notes.

Archive

Older versions, retired drafts, superseded records, historical reports, and frozen snapshots are preserved.

Research Standards

Quality Principles

Evidence First

Claims should be grounded in sources, reproducible observations, measured results, or clearly labeled assumptions.

Clear Scope

Every paper, report, disclosure, dataset, and analysis package should define scope, limits, assumptions, and exclusions.

Reproducibility

Where practical, research should include methods, tooling, versions, hashes, manifests, references, and repeatable workflows.

Responsible Disclosure

Security work should avoid reckless publication and support safe remediation where affected parties can be contacted.

Bitcoin-Native Integrity

Payments, access, archival records, publication flows, and commercial research products should align with Bitcoin-native settlement.

Open Where Possible

Public-interest findings, open-source tools, public datasets, and educational materials should be released openly where appropriate.
