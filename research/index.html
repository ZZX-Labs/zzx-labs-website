Standards

Research Quality Principles

Evidence First

Claims should be grounded in sources, reproducible observations, measured results, or clearly labeled assumptions.

Clear Scope

Every report, paper, disclosure, dataset, and analysis package should define scope, limits, assumptions, and exclusions.

Reproducibility

Where practical, research should include methods, tooling, versions, hashes, manifests, references, and repeatable workflows.

Responsible Disclosure

Security work should avoid reckless publication and support safe remediation where affected parties can be contacted.

Bitcoin-Native Integrity

Payments, access, archival records, and publication flows should align with Bitcoin-native settlement and verification.

Open Where Possible

Public-interest findings, open-source tools, public datasets, and educational materials should be released openly where appropriate.
