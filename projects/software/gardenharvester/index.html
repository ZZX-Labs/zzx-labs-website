gardenharvester

Radio Garden stream harvesting & tagging: schedule recordings, auto-title, segment, and tag to MP3/Opus with station & show metadata.

Open Project View on GitHub

For personal time-shifting and research. Honors robots/network limits, supports per-station consent lists, and avoids abusive scraping.

Overview

gardenharvester automates capture of Radio Garden streams with precise scheduling, silence/voice detection for clean cuts, and rich tagging. It normalizes audio, embeds ID3/Opus tags, writes cue sheets/chapters, and organizes recordings into date- and station-based libraries. Outputs M3U/PLS playlists and JSONL manifests for reproducible archives.

        Highlights

        • Station capture: Record from Radio Garden endpoints you configure (by URL or station ID).
        • Scheduling: Cron-like timers, per-station calendars, and skip windows to avoid peak hours.
        • Smart segmentation: Silence/VAD cutters, max-length rotators, optional replaygain/normalization.
        • Metadata & tagging: Auto title with Station · Show · Date/Time, ID3/Opus tags, chapters, cue.
        • Formats: MP3 (LAME), Opus (libopus), FLAC optional; WAV intermediates when needed.
        • Library & manifests: Station/date foldering, M3U/PLS, JSONL manifests with hashes for integrity.
        • Ethics & safety: Rate limits, retries with backoff/jitter, allow/deny station lists, user-agent ID.
        • Viewer/API: Lightweight local web UI for schedules, logs, and library browsing.

        Legal & Fair Use

        Use only where lawful and permitted by the station’s terms. This tool is intended for personal time-shifting, research, and interoperability. Respect rightsholder policies, do not rebroadcast without permission, and comply with local law.
